Overview

A digital-native coliving project rethinking the modern city

Cabin is a woodsy, digital-native coliving project trying to rethink the modern city with a touch of crypto governance — providing online communities a place to come together to live and work IRL across a network of privately owned properties around the world.

Cabin's vision embraces blockchain technology to create an IRL RPG — a financially sustainable way for the community to continue to grow, while simultaneously providing a value and vibe alignment check for people who want to colive in Cabin neighborhoods.

I collaborated with the team to design and build a web app where Cabin's community can view rich member profiles, discover new neighborhoods to visit, and keep track of activity happening across the entire community.

Process

Designing for a community that builds itself

Designing for a decentralized community is fundamentally different from designing for a traditional product. The users are also the builders — so the interface needed to reflect their agency and foster participation, not just consume content.

01

Member Profiles

Rich profile pages that let community members express their identity, skills, and coliving interests — the social foundation of the platform.

02

Neighborhood Discovery

A browsable map and listing experience for Cabin's growing network of properties — helping members find places to visit and stay.

03

Community Activity

A live feed of happenings across the Cabin community — projects, proposals, events, and conversations surfaced in one place.
